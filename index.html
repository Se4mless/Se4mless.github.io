The Nintendo Skibiditiobox was a home video game console developed and manufactured by Nintendo. It was released on November 17, 2023, as part of the ninth generation of video game consoles. The Skibiditiobox succeeded the Nintendo Switch and introduced several technological innovations, including 8K resolution support and adaptive controller feedback.

History

Development of the Skibiditiobox began in late 2020 under the codename "Project EchoBeat." Nintendo aimed to create a system that bridged traditional console experiences with cloud-integrated gaming. The console was unveiled during a Nintendo Direct in June 2023.

Features

The Skibiditiobox featured a custom 10-core ARM processor, an advanced GPU branded as the SkibidiGPU-X, and 16 GB of LPDDR6 memory. It supported physical media via proprietary SkibidiDiscs, as well as digital downloads through the Skibiditi Online storefront. Its signature controller, the JoySkibidi Pro, included haptic feedback, motion tracking, and voice-activated controls.

Games

The console launched with several first-party titles, including:

  • Mario Multiverse Brawl
  • Zelda: Crystal of Eternity
  • Skibidi Kart X
  • Metroid Prime: Resurgence

It also supported backwards compatibility with select Nintendo Switch titles via a downloadable emulator patch.

Reception and Legacy

The Skibiditiobox received positive critical acclaim for its performance and software library, but its high launch price and limited third-party support contributed to a relatively short market lifespan. Production officially ended in March 2025, making it one of Nintendo’s shortest-supported home consoles.
